
emergence of mixed-of-experts (MoE) models has led to significant advancements in the field of natural language processing. These innovative models aspire to capture larger language capacities and computational efficiency by selectively activating a smaller subset of experts per token, rather than treating all experts equally.

The key concept behind MoE lies in the idea that not every expert need be active for a given set of tokens. Instead, an appropriate routing mechanism can give more importance to fewer, relevant experts while downscaling the rest to manageable levels. This allows MoE models to handle larger models without having to process all parameters proportionally when they're feasible and maintain a large total parameter count.

The resurging interest in MoE goes back to the 1990s, but its resurgence can be attributed mainly due to transformer-based language models. The key lies in sparse expert routing strategies that allow for competitive or superior performance compared to dense models of equivalent computational cost.
