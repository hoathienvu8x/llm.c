The 1982–1983 period followed the rise to dominance in theoretical mathematics by researchers from Harvard University, Princeton University (PNP), Cambridge Analytica and Washington State Univ., Berkeley Lab, UMass Amherst College and Carnegie Mellon School of Engineering . Until 1986, most journals focused on computational models had assumed an analytic model based upon Turing's work rather than his own understanding of the nature or function in complex biological phenomena. Consequently these researchers held out hope for something much more appealing: predictive modeling from theoretical mathematics by leading theorists who testified before Congress and government officials investigating extraterrestrials . The 1980s, however delayed even this effort because it was followed by an acknowledgment in 1986 that computational models must exclude the assumption of analytic model­ing under certain limitations.
