This section describes the early developments in artificial intelligence research, including its development by Turing during his lifetime. The first of these was at MIT's Laboratory on Artificial Intelligence (LAI). In 1962, it became known as the LCA-MIT project to study how a group of scientists from different fields could work together for one goal: developing new approaches to solving problems that were difficult or impossible in an artificial world. This led to Turing and his colleagues working at MIT's Laboratory on Artificial Intelligence (LAI), which was renamed by their successors, the LCA-MIT project after its title . The team developed a series of computer models designed specifically for research purposes: they used mathematical algorithms such as Riemannian convolutional networks; orGANs that were based on reinforcement learning.
