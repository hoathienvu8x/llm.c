Tell me about Alan Turing. He was a British mathematician, computer scientist, and logician who is widely considered the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. Born on June 23, 1912, in London, Turing showed exceptional mathematical ability from an early age. He studied at King's College, Cambridge, and later at Princeton University, where he earned his PhD. During World War II, Turing played a pivotal role in breaking the German Enigma cipher at Bletchley Park, which significantly contributed to the Allied victory.
