He also proposed the Turing test as a measure of artificial intelligence's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human. Tragically, due to his homosexuality being illegal at the time in Britain, Turing was persecuted and prosecuted by authorities, leading to his untimely death by suicide in 1954. Despite this, Turing's contributions to computer science and mathematics continue to be celebrated and studied worldwide.
