He also proposed the "Turing test," a measure of a machine's ability to demonstrate intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, human intelligence. In addition, Turing made significant contributions in the field of cryptanalysis and was a pioneer in artificial intelligence and moral philosophy. Tragically, Turing's life was cut short when he died by suicide on June 7, 1954, at the age of 41, after being persecuted for his homosexuality. His legacy continues to be celebrated and recognized for its immense contributions to the field of computer science and beyond.
