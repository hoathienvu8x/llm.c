After the war, Turing continued his work in cryptography and also focused on mathematical biology, specifically morphogenesis, which is the process by which organisms develop their shape and form. He published several papers on this topic before his untimely death in 1954 at the age of 41.

Despite Turing's significant contributions to mathematics and computer science, he faced persecution due to his homosexuality, which was illegal in England at the time. In 1952, he was arrested and convicted of gross indecency. As a result, he underwent hormonal treatment as an alternative to imprisonment.
